Prompt

"[The Data Services Librarian] is a newly created position in the UMass Amherst Libraries. As such, what is your vision of the top priorities?"

Briefly: An introduction to Data Services

  • Not just for the sciences! Support for all research & scholarship.
  • Support throughout the research lifecycle.
  • Help create policy, monitor compliance, guide infrastructure.
  • New topic for many faculty, communities, funders, publishers.

Briefly: An introduction to Data Services

  • Builds on existing library relationships & library skills.
  • Builds on existing conceptual frameworks -- like information literacy.
  • Libraries have been organizing information for a long time! We have a lot to share, but a lot to do to be at the right tables.

Briefly: An introduction to Data Services

"Librarians working in research data management need to add value, not work, to a researcher's project." (Henderson, 2016).

Vision of top priorities

  • Education, support, & how the Libraries fit in with current campus offerings.
  • Build partnerships & bring in stakeholders.
  • Examine our infrastructure and services & make a plan for continuous assessment.

Top priorities

Education, support, & how the Libraries fit in with current campus offerings

Avoid redundancy!

E.g., methods: Institute for Social Science Research (ISSR) & Center for Research on Families (CRF); some software: ISSR; study design: ISSR, Biostats Consulting Center (BCC), Statistical Consulting & Collaborative Services (SCCS); statistical consulting: ISSR, BCC, SCCS; data analysis: SCCS...

Top priorities

Build partnerships & bring in stakeholders

Currently:

  1. Maintain relationships within the libraries, with institutes and schools across campus. Top partnerships include Graduate School, College of Natural Sciences, Office of Research Development.
  2. Establishing new partnerships, role of serendipity.

Top priorities

Build partnerships & bring in stakeholders

Next steps:

  1. Leverage current partnerships.
  2. Create a team of library staff invested in RDM support and exploration (Data Working Group or otherwise).
  3. Find opportunities for parallel growth.

Top priorities

Examine our infrastructure and services...

What do we offer? What should we offer? Where are we siloed? Where do we collaborate?

What can we improve?

Top priorities

...and make a plan for continuous assessment

When new services are developed, how will we know? When we need to develop new services, how do we understand the needs of our community? When do we need to leverage cross-organizational infrastructure? Who is doing ____ already?

Top priorities

Infrastructure, services, and assessment

Next steps:

  1. Understand what we want to assess, and how.
  2. Create our own Data Management Plan for Data Services, other policies.
  3. Thoughtfully collect data - every data point should be of use.

Vision building towards...

A comprehensive, straightforward, centralized, patron-based approach to Data Services and research support -- across all disciplines.

Integrate data literacy skills across the curriculum -- so we graduate data-literate students (e.g., Sapp Nelson, 2017).

Making data-based decisions in Data Services.

Potential challenges

All priorities are interrelated, and build off of one another - need to be flexible if one part fails.

As reach and services grow, so too will complexity and need for clear communication.

Practice what we teach: good data management in-house.

Sustainable growth.

Community adoption.

Finally, Data Services can...

... add value, integrate assessment, improve reproducibility, foster innovation, further engrain the libraries in the research lifecycle, and facilitate openness and communication in scholarship. But, we need to do so while being considerate and cognizant of existing work and workflows.
