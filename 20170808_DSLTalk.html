Prompt

"[The Data Services Librarian] is a newly created position in the UMass Amherst Libraries. As such, what is your vision of the top priorities?"

Briefly: An introduction to Data Services

  • Not just for the sciences! Support for all research.
  • Support throughout the research lifecycle - from ideas to discovery, planning to archiving and curating, documentation to visualization.
  • Help create policy, monitor compliance, develop infrastructure.
  • New topic for many faculty, communities, funders, publishers.

Briefly: An introduction to Data Services

  • Expands on existing liaison relationships & library skills.
  • Builds on existing conceptual frameworks -- like information literacy.
  • Libraries have been organizing information for a long time! We have a lot to share, but a lot to do to be at the right tables.

Briefly: An introduction to Data Services

"Librarians working in research data management need to add value, not work, to a researcher's project." (Henderson, 2016).

Vision of top priorities

  • Education, support, & how the Libraries fit in with current campus offerings.
  • Build partnerships & bring in stakeholders.
  • Examine our infrastructure and services & make a plan for continuous assessment.

Potential challenges

All priorities are interrelated, and build off of one another.

Practice what we teach: good data management in-house.

Community adoption.

Vision building towards...

A comprehensive, straightforward, centralized, patron-based approach to Data Services and research support -- across all disciplines.

Integrate data literacy skills across the curriculum -- so we graduate data-literate students.

Making data-based decisions in Data Services.

Finally, Data Services can...

... add value, integrate assessment, improve reproducibility, and facilitate openness and communication in scholarship, but we need to do so thoughtfully and cognizant of existing work and workflows.
